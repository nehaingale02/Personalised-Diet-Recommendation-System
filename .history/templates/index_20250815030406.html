02

What is Kapha ?

+
Kapha Dosha in Ayurveda is composed of earth (Prithvi) and water (Jala) elements, providing stability, lubrication, and nourishment to the body. It governs strength, immunity, and emotional balance, residing mainly in the chest, throat, stomach, and joints. Kapha's qualities include heavy, cool, oily, and stable attributes. When balanced, it promotes endurance and calmness, but excess Kapha leads to lethargy, weight gain, and congestion, while deficiency causes weakness and dryness. To balance Kapha,Ayurveda recommends warm, light foods, exercise, and stimulating activities, while avoiding excessive sleep and inactivity. Herbs like ginger and black pepper help maintain equilibrium.
03

What is Vata ?

+
In Ayurveda, Vata is composed of the air (Vayu) and ether (Akasha) elements, governing movement, circulation, and nervous system function. It controls breathing, digestion, and mental agility. Vata is light, dry, cold, mobile, and irregular in nature. When balanced, it fosters creativity, enthusiasm, and flexibility. However, excess Vata can lead to anxiety, dryness, digestive issues, and restlessness, while deficiency may cause sluggishness and confusion. To balance Vata, Ayurveda recommends warm, moist foods, grounding activities, and a regular routine. Herbs like ashwagandha and sesame oil massages help restore balance.
04

What is Pitta ?

+
Pitta Dosha in Ayurveda is composed of fire (Agni) and water (Jala) elements, governing metabolism, digestion, and energy transformation. It controls body temperature, hunger, thirst, and intelligence. Pitta is hot, sharp, oily, and intense in nature. When balanced, it promotes courage, intelligence, and strong digestion. However, excess Pitta can lead to inflammation, acidity, anger, and skin issues, while deficiency may cause poor digestion and low energy. To balance Pitta, Ayurveda recommends cooling foods, moderate exercise, and stress management. Herbs like aloe vera and cooling spices help maintain equilibrium.
